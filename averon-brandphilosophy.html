Why AVERON Was Created

O-01

AVERON started from a personal problem: persistent inner dryness. No matter how premium or highly recommended a product was, it often felt like it sat on the surface — never truly integrating with the skin.

Through professional consultation, the insight became clear: when the skin barrier is compromised and dehydration is persistent, the skin struggles to retain moisture and becomes less resilient. In that state, even well-formulated actives may feel inconsistent — not because the formula is “bad,” but because the foundation is unstable.

That is the core philosophy of AVERON: foundation before performance. We prioritize barrier integrity and hydration retention first, so the skin is comfortable, stable, and ready — in clinic protocols and in daily routines.

  • Barrier-first formulation logic for sensitive and easily disrupted skin
  • Hydration retention and comfort as the baseline
  • Designed to layer smoothly within professional protocols
  • Built for daily use without heaviness or disruption

Scientific Basis

AVERON’s “foundation before performance” philosophy aligns with established skin-barrier science. Below are concise references partners can review for barrier integrity, hydration metrics, and moisturizer-driven barrier support.
S-01

Barrier integrity is central to hydration stability

  • The stratum corneum functions as a semi-permeable barrier that limits water loss.
  • The “bricks and mortar” concept describes corneocytes (bricks) and intercellular lipids (mortar) supporting barrier function.
  • When barrier components are disrupted, water loss increases and skin becomes less resilient.
Ref: PubMed: 17313091 (Two compartment model; bricks & mortar; barrier role)

TEWL is a widely used objective barrier metric

  • Transepidermal water loss (TEWL) is widely used to assess barrier function in research and clinical contexts.
  • Higher TEWL generally indicates a more compromised barrier.
Ref: J Invest Dermatol (TEWL measurement review)
Ref: PubMed: 19799976 (TEWL as water vapor flux; barrier characterization)

Hydration can be measured; barrier support depends on formulation

  • Skin surface hydration is commonly assessed using capacitance (e.g., Corneometer) and related instruments.
  • Moisturizer composition can influence barrier outcomes over time (e.g., TEWL changes can vary by formula).
Ref: PubMed: 27327056 (Hydration instruments + TEWL in moisturizer evaluation)
Ref: PubMed: 17300239 (Long-term moisturizer RCT; barrier outcomes depend on composition)

NMF relates to stratum corneum hydration capacity

  • Natural Moisturizing Factor (NMF) components contribute to hydration within the stratum corneum.
  • Reduced NMF is linked with reduced hydration capacity in specific contexts (e.g., filaggrin genotype studies).
Ref: Br J Dermatol (NMF as biomarker; filaggrin genotype)
